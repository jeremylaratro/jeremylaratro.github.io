About Me:

I graduated in August 2022 with a B.Sc. in Integrative Animal Biology from the University of South Florida. During my time in undergrad, I took a variety of essential classes, including pre-calculus and trigonometry, calculus I, physics I & II, general chemistry I & II, biology I & II, cell biology, genetics, organic chemistry I & II, and many more. My favorite classes were physics II and organic chemistry.

Upon the start of the pandemic, I found many passions and interests which I simply had never been exposed to prior, such as electrical engineering and programming. The pandemic served as a bridge into these new interests via the extra free time and isolation.
Specifically, I happened to come upon amateur radio and Arduino, and from there I continued to grow and vigorously expand my interest and knowledge of circuits, software and programming, radio and antenna theory, and general electronics.

In the time since, I've built up a repertoire of projects, put together a versatile and functional electronics bench with an oscilloscope, VNA, spectrum analyzer, TDR meter, SMT soldering station, as well as a fully functional radio station in which I have contacted people from every continent and nearly 100 unique countries.

My current goal is to continue learning and break into either the cybersecurity or electrical engineering field. I'm working towards a cyberecurity certificate at the moment, and following a diversified learning pathway which includes both fundamental theory and background along with practical application. Using ethical hacking channels such as tryhackme, I've become familiar with a wide variety of tools and techniques, including metasploit framework, burp suite, hashcat, nmap, netcat, ffuf, gobuster, wpscan, sqlmap, maltego, whatweb, binwalk, along with concepts such as SSRF, XSS, privilege escalation, reverse shells, network enumeration, and many, many more. I'm currently ranked within the top 10% of users on tryhackme.com.
