About Me:

I'm currently a student at the University of South Florida. My Major is Integrative Animal Biology, and while I love this field, I discovered my passion for technology during the beginning of the pandemic, when spare time was plentiful. I was first introduced to radio and soon after, branched out into various aspects of electronics. In the time since then, I've built up a sizeable amateur radio station, designed my own antennas for it using antenna modeling software, and learned to use a VNA, oscilloscope, signal analyzer, TDR, and various other tools. After being intrigued by RF circuits and RF in general, I became interested in learning about circuit design, and did many projects using Arduino (which was also my first introduction to programming) as well as other microcontrollers, such as STM, ARM, and Raspberry Pi. This then led me to designing my own circuits and learning to create PCB layouts in KiCAD, many of which I've sent off for professional manufacturing. After one of my prototypes failed, due to a simple error in the layout, I sought to find a way to implement and debug my prototypes before sending out the GRBR files for production, and with more accuracy and reliability than a breadboard. So, I decided I wanted to try two methods of creating PCBs at home, the first being mechanical etching, and the second chemical etching. I built a mini-CNC machine for mechanical etching, and also learned about chemical etching using photosensitive stencils and ferric chloride. Since then, I've finished many projects, seriously started learning python, contributed to various open-source projects, and also completed a few personal organic chemistry projects.

I have also started a small business in that time, in which I program radios, scanners, and also do computer/electronic device repair.

I truly enjoy learning about new things, and make an effort to learn something new every day. To me, expanding my knowledge is one of the most important things I can do, and I am always looking for ways to improve myself and my skills. There are few things more exciting to me than creating a working circuit or program or finding the issue in a broken circuit and restoring it back to full functionality.
